üö® Common Warning Signs

Urgent Language

Phrases like "Act now!", "Limited time!", "Your account will be closed!" are designed to create panic and bypass critical thinking.

Generic Greetings

"Dear Customer" or "Dear User" instead of your actual name often indicates mass-sent phishing emails.

Suspicious Links

URLs that don't match the claimed sender or use URL shorteners to hide the real destination.

Poor Grammar/Spelling

Professional organizations typically have proper grammar. Multiple errors may indicate a scam.

Unexpected Attachments

Unsolicited attachments, especially .exe, .zip, or .doc files from unknown senders.

Requests for Information

Legitimate companies rarely ask for passwords, SSN, or credit card info via email.
